Rebuilding

The summer of 1998 brought an abrupt end to the Michael Jordan era. The Bulls were on the verge of being too old and unable to compete. So they traded all their old players for new players with potential. In that season, the Bulls scored 49 points to set an NBA record for the fewest points in a game in the shot clock era. For the next three seasons they were tied for the worst team and the second to worst team in the NBA.
